The fix? Automated reporting that pulls from Meta Ads, Google Ads, Klaviyo, and Shopify into one dashboard — updated in real-time.

No copy-paste. No Friday night slide decks. No $27k/year leak.

If your team still spends 5+ hours on weekly reports, you're leaving margin on the table.

## Post 2: The SOP Blindspot

**Format:** Carousel (3-4 slides recommended)
**Hook type:** Diagnostic question

---

**Headline on first slide/card:**
Where do your agency's processes live?

**Body:**

I ask every agency founder I talk to this question.

Most answers fall into one of three buckets:

🟢 "They're in ClickUp/Notion — we document everything."
🟡 "They exist... somewhere. In Slack messages, old emails, people's heads."
🔴 "What SOPs?"

Here's the problem with yellow and red:

Every undocumented process creates a hidden tax on your agency.

1. New hires take 3x longer to ramp up
2. Client work varies in quality depending on who does it
3. You can't scale without the founder answering the same questions weekly
4. When someone leaves, their knowledge leaves with them

The agencies I work with aim for an SOP score of 8/10 or higher.

Most start at 2-3/10.

The gap between 2 and 8 is the difference between an agency that runs itself and an agency that runs its founder.

Start small: Document ONE process this week. Your client reporting workflow. Or your lead intake. Just one.

That's the first step from 🔴 to 🟡.

---

**Slides format (carousel):**
- Slide 1: The question + 3 buckets (red/yellow/green visual)
- Slide 2: The hidden tax (4 bullet points)
- Slide 3: The gap (2/10 → 8/10)
- Slide 4: Start small call-to-action

## Post 3: Lead Response — The 5-Minute Test

**Format:** Single text post
**Hook type:** Data point

---

Average agency lead response time: 3-24 hours.

If you don't have a system, you're probably in that range.

Here's what happens in those 3-24 hours:

1. The lead fills out a form or sends an email
2. It sits in an inbox until someone notices
3. The right person is busy, in a meeting, or off for the day
4. By the time someone responds, the lead's momentum is gone

Compare that to agencies using AI-powered lead qualification:

Response in under 60 seconds. 24/7. Weekend. Holiday. 2 AM.

The lead gets a reply immediately. They get qualified. If they're a fit, a call gets booked before your team even wakes up.

I'm not saying you need a full AI SDR from day one.

But if your lead response time is measured in hours (or days), you're losing deals you never knew existed.

Start here: What happens to a lead between "form submitted" and "human reads it"?

If the answer is "nothing," that's your first automation opportunity.
